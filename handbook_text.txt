Section 1: Introduction to Handbook

* The handbook provides general guidelines to familiarize employees with the Organization’s HR policies and procedures \r\n.

* It acts as a guide to assist employees in becoming familiar with their privileges and obligations \r\n.

* Individual copies are provided upon employment, and all employees are expected to abide by them \r\n.

* The content of the handbook is confidential and belongs to the organization \r\n.

* Employees are not allowed to print, copy, disclose, or use any part of it without HR team approval \r\n


Section 2: School Hierarchy & Categories

* The Academic Hierarchy flows from Principal to Academic EA, Academic Coordinators, and then Teachers \r\n

* The Operations Hierarchy includes the Operations Manager, Campus Incharge (CIC), Accountant, and Ancillary Staff.\r\n

* Campus Incharge (CIC) has a dual reporting relationship, including a line to the Security Head.\r\n

* Teaching Staff includes Teachers, Senior Faculty, Librarians, Lab Assistants, and Counselors.\r\n

* Admin Staff includes CIC, TIC, EA, Admission Counselors (PRM), Store Managers, and Accountants.\r\n


Section 3: Recruitment Process

* A TA SPOC (Single Point of Contact) is assigned to each hiring manager once a manpower requisition is raised.

* Recruitment steps involve CV screening, interviews with the Hiring Manager, and final selection.

* The Onboarding team handles induction, appointment letters, biometric access, and Email ID creation.

* Mandatory documents include E-Aadhar Card, PAN Card, and Bank Details.

* Employees covered under PF must submit an updated Aadhar card showing the full Date of Birth within 15 days of joining.

* Failure to submit the Aadhar card will result in salary being placed on hold and exit from the system.


Section 4: Codes Relevant to Employees

* The ERP/Employee Code serves as the primary identification for punch in Eduvate.

* The code always starts with the Year of Joining (e.g., 2021xxxxxxx).

* This unique code is required for all formal communications with the HR department.

* It is the unique identifier for logging into the Eduvate ERP platform.


Section 5: Probation and Confirmation

* The mandatory probation period for Admin and Marketing Staff is 12 Months.

* The mandatory probation period for Teaching Staff and Deputy Leaders is 6 Months.

* The organization reserves the right to extend the probation period up to 3 years based on performance.

* Employees are deemed confirmed unless a written extension of probation is provided.

* There is no difference in leave rules between confirmed and non-confirmed staff members.


Section 6: Attendance Policy

* Employees must daily punch in and out on the biometric machine to reflect their attendance.

* Staff must strictly follow their assigned shift timings.

* A 15-minute buffer period is provided from the start of the shift.

* If an employee punches in 15 minutes after the shift start time, the system automatically marks them as half-day absent.

* Attendance should be checked on GreytHR every 3-4 days to identify any technical issues.

* Only 2 attendance regularizations (e.g., forgot to punch) are allowed per month.


Section 7: Leave Policy

* The Leave Cycle runs from 26th May of the current year to 25th May of the next year.

* Casual Leave (CL): 11 days per cycle, credited on a pro-rata basis (6 in June, 5 in December).

* Maximum of 3 Casual Leaves are permitted in a single month.

* Maternity Leave (ML): Available after one year of continuous service for up to 2 children.

* Term Vacations: Paid vacations for teaching staff only (Summer, Diwali/Dussehra, Christmas).

* Sandwich Policy: Taking leave both before and after a holiday or weekend causes those holidays to be counted as leaves.

* Vacation Sandwich: Taking leave the day before a vacation starts or the day of reopening results in the entire vacation being treated as LOP.


Section 8: Salary and Payroll

* The Payroll Cycle runs from the 26th of the current month to the 25th of the next month.

* Salaries are released on or before the 10th of the following month.

* If an employee joins after the 22nd of the month, they are added to the next month's payroll cycle with arrears.

* PF (Provident Fund) is mandatory for employees with a basic salary equal to or below INR 15,000 per month.

* Salary Advance: Employees can request up to one month's gross after 6 months of service, repayable in 3 installments.

* Staff Children Concession: Employees are eligible for fee concessions for their children after completing probation.


Section 9: Performance Appraisal

* Eligibility: Employees who joined on or before 30th September of the academic year.

* Increments are based on feedback from reporting managers, school heads, and department heads.

* All increments for teaching and admin staff are effective from 26th May and paid along with the July salary.

* Evaluation for teachers includes documented lesson observation reports throughout the year.


Section 10: Reimbursement Policy**

* All travel and reimbursement requests must be pre-approved by the reporting manager and HOD.

* Employees must choose the lowest available airfare for business travel.

* Daily food expenses are reimbursed according to the Entitlement Matrix based on employee cadre.

* Expense claims without scanned copies of original bills will not be considered for reimbursement.

* Local conveyance claims must be submitted using the specific Local Conveyance Claim Form.
